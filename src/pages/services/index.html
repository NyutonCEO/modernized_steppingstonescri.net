Services

Each service below links to a full overview, expectations, and FAQs.

Individual Outpatient Therapy

Individual Outpatient Therapy is a specialized treatment that is personalized to the needs of the individual and recognizes the importance of the individual. We provide quality treatment in areas of anger management, mood disorders, anxiety disorders, attention deficit hyperactivity disorders (ADHD), behavioral disorders, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), stress management, substance abuse disorders, gambling addiction disorders, eating disorders, and obsessive-compulsive disorders.

Learn more

Substance Abuse Counseling Outpatient Treatment

Substance Abuse Counseling Outpatient Treatment (SACOT) is a structured individual and group addiction activities and services that are provided at an outpatient program designed to assist adults and adolescents to begin recovery and learn skills for recovery maintenance. The program is offered at least 4 hours a day, at least 4 days a week, no more than 7 days a week with no more than 2 consecutive days between offered services, and distinguishes between those individuals needing more than 20 hours of structured services per week.

Learn more

Substance Abuse Intensive Outpatient Program

Substance Abuse Intensive Outpatient Program (SAIOP) is a structured individual and group addiction activities and services that are provided at an outpatient program with the goals of assisting adults and adolescents to begin recovery and learn life skills for recovery maintenance. The program is offered at least 3 hours a day, at least 3 days a week, with no more than 2 consecutive days between offered services, and distinguishes between those individuals needing no more than 15 hours of structured services per week.

Learn more

Case Management

Case Management is a service for adults, women who are pregnant and children age three (3) and older who have a serious emotional disturbance, mental illness, or a substance abuse related disorder. Case Management assists recipients in gaining access to necessary care and medical, behavioral, social, housing and other services appropriate to their needs.

Learn more

Primary Care

Stepping Stones Primary Care is committed to being the main point of contact for continuing care for patients within our health care system. We are dedicated to health promotion, disease prevention, health maintenance, counseling, patient education, diagnosis, and treatment.
